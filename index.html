Do You Qualify? Eligible Conditions

You may qualify for disability designation if you have a condition that significantly restricts your daily activities and is expected to last at least 2 years or is likely to recur.

Common Qualifying Conditions:

Mental Health Conditions:

  • Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)
  • Depression (Major Depressive Disorder)
  • Bipolar Disorder
  • Schizophrenia
  • Anxiety Disorders (GAD, Panic Disorder, PTSD, OCD)
  • Borderline Personality Disorder (BPD)
  • ADHD (severe cases affecting daily function)

Physical Conditions:

  • Chronic Pain (Fibromyalgia, Back Pain, etc.)
  • Multiple Sclerosis (MS)
  • Cerebral Palsy
  • Muscular Dystrophy
  • Spinal Cord Injuries
  • Amputation or Limb Disabilities
  • Severe Arthritis
  • Chronic Fatigue Syndrome (ME/CFS)

Neurological Conditions:

  • Epilepsy
  • Parkinson's Disease
  • Traumatic Brain Injury (TBI)
  • Stroke (with lasting effects)
  • Dementia or Cognitive Impairments

Chronic Illnesses:

  • Diabetes (Type 1 or severe Type 2)
  • Heart Disease
  • Kidney Disease
  • Liver Disease
  • Cancer (ongoing treatment or effects)
  • HIV/AIDS
  • Crohn's Disease or Ulcerative Colitis
  • Severe Asthma or COPD

Sensory Disabilities:

  • Blindness or Low Vision
  • Deafness or Hearing Loss

Learning & Developmental Disabilities:

  • Intellectual Disabilities
  • Fetal Alcohol Spectrum Disorder (FASD)
  • Learning Disabilities (severe cases)

Note: This is not an exhaustive list. If you have any medical condition that significantly limits your daily activities, you may still qualify. Talk to your doctor about whether your condition meets the criteria.

Key Requirement: Your condition must be expected to last at least 2 years OR continue indefinitely OR be likely to recur. Your doctor must confirm this on Form HR2883.
